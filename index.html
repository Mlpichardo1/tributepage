PAUL OF TARSUS

The Apostle Paul


"One of the most fascinating, important, and controversial figures in the religious history of the West, Paul the Apostle continues to find champions and detractors, sometimes in surprising places." --- Professor Johnson.

TIMELINE OF PAUL'S LIFE →

  1. 6 A.D.Born a Roman citizen to Jewish parents in Tarsus (in modern eastern Turkey)
  2. 20–30 A.D.Studies Torah in Jerusalem with Gamaliel; becomes a Pharisee
  3. 30–33 A.D.Persecutes followers of Jesus of Nazareth in Jerusalem and Judea
  4. 33–36 A.D.Converted on the way to Damascus; spends three years in Arabia; returns to Damascus to preach Jesus as Messiah
  5. 36 A.D.Flees Damascus because of persecution; visits Jerusalem and meets with the apostles
  6. 36–44 A.D.Preaches in Tarsus and surrounding region
  7. 44–46 A.D.Invited by Barnabas to teach in Antioch and also visits Jerusalem to bring a famine relief offering
  8. 47–48 A.D.First missionary journey with Barnabas, to Cyprus and Galatia
  9. 49 A.D.At the Council of Jerusalem, Paul argues successfully that Gentile Christians need not follow Jewish law; returns to Antioch; confronts Peter over question of Jewish law
  10. 49–52 A.D.Second missionary journey with Silas, through Asia Minor and Greece; settles in Corinth; writes letters to Thessalonians
  11. 52 A.D.Visits Jerusalem and Antioch briefly; begins third missionary journey
  12. 52–57 A.D.Stays in Ephesus; writes the letters to Galatians and Corinthians. Travels through Greece and possibly Illyricum (modern Yugoslavia); writes letter to Romans
  13. 57–60 A.D. Returns to Jerusalem and arrested; imprisoned at Caesarea. Appears before Festus and appeals to Caesar; voyage to Rome
  14. 60–62 A.D. Under house arrest at Rome; writes letters to Philippians, Ephesians, Colossians, and Philemon
  15. 62–64 A.D. Released; journeys to Spain?; writes letters to Timothy and Titus
  16. 64 A.D.Returns to Rome; martyred during this period of early persecution of Christians

BIO

Paul was a Greek-speaking Jew from Asia Minor. His birthplace, Tarsus, was a major city in eastern Cilicia, a region that had been made part of the Roman province of Syria by the time of Paul’s adulthood. Two of the main cities of Syria, Damascus and Antioch, played a prominent part in his life and letters. Although the exact date of his birth is unknown, he was active as a missionary in the 40s and 50s of the 1st century ad. From this it may be inferred that he was born about the same time as Jesus (c. 4 bc) or a little later. He was converted to faith in Jesus Christ about ad 33, and he died, probably in Rome, circa ad 62–64.
